Koh Phangan

The closest island on the route and the one with the widest range — quiet coves on one coast, full-on energy on the other.

~12 km

Two coastlines, two completely different islands

Koh Phangan is best known internationally for its Full Moon Party scene on Haad Rin Beach, and if that's what you're after, it delivers. But that's one beach on one side of an island that's mostly forest, limestone hills, and a coastline of quiet bays most visitors never see from a boat.

An Ocean United charter here lets you pick which version of the island you want. Run the eastern coast for the energy and the beach scene, or work the quieter northern and western bays for the kind of swimming spots that don't show up in the party photos. Being the closest island on our route, it's also the easiest to combine with a relaxed half-day on the water.

What to expect

01

A short, easy crossing — the shortest of the three outer-island routes from Koh Samui.

02

Real variety along the coastline, from built-up beach clubs to forested, undeveloped shoreline.

03

Calm anchorages well suited to swimming stops and a slower-paced day.

04

If your dates land near a full moon, expect Haad Rin to be considerably busier than the rest of the island.

On the water

Because the crossing is short, Koh Phangan works well as either a relaxed half-day trip or as one leg of a longer day that also touches Koh Samui's coastline. Tell us what kind of day you want — beach-club energy or quiet coves — and we'll plan the route around it.
