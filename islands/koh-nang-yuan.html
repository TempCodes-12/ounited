Koh Nang Yuan

Three islets, one sandbar holding them together, and a viewpoint that's been photographed more than almost anywhere else in Thailand — for good reason.

Distance from Harbor
~62 km
Typical Visit
Half day stop
Best For
Viewpoint & snorkeling
Water Conditions
Clear, shallow reef

Small island, outsized reputation

Koh Nang Yuan is technically three separate islets, held together by a sandbar that appears at low tide and nearly disappears at high tide. It sits a short distance off Koh Tao's northwest coast, and it's been used in enough tourism photography that you've probably seen it without knowing the name.

The reputation is earned. A short, steep climb up granite boulders leads to a viewpoint looking straight down over the sandbar and all three islets at once — one of the more genuinely striking views in this part of the Gulf of Thailand. Below the surface, the shallow reef around the island is some of the healthiest near Koh Tao, with calm, protected snorkeling close to shore.

What to expect

01

A short, steep climb to the viewpoint over loose boulders — manageable, but wear real shoes, not flip-flops.

02

Shallow, calm reef water close to the beach, well suited to snorkeling for any experience level.

03

A small island that draws a steady stream of day-trip boats — expect company, especially around midday.

04

Limited shade on the beach itself, so plan accordingly if you're spending a few hours.

On the water

Given how close it sits to Koh Tao, this is almost always run as a two-stop day — diving or open-water snorkeling at Koh Tao, then a shorter hop over to Koh Nang Yuan for the viewpoint and the calmer reef. On its own, it works well as a shorter half-day stop if the viewpoint and sandbar are the main goal.
