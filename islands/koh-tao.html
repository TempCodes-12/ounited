Koh Tao

Thailand's busiest diving island, and the one most guests build their whole trip around. Clear water, reef walls, and a reputation that holds up.

Turtle Island, by reputation and by name

Koh Tao earned its nickname honestly — the island's outline from above resembles a turtle, and the water around it is some of the clearest in the Gulf of Thailand. It's the furthest of the four routes from home port, which is exactly why people make the trip: this is where the diving gets serious.

The island itself is small and walkable, with a handful of bays worth anchoring in depending on conditions that day. Most of what makes Koh Tao worth the crossing happens beneath the surface, but the coastline above it — granite boulders, narrow coves, a skyline with almost no high-rises — is its own reason to slow down.

What to expect

01

Visibility that regularly runs 15–20 meters, with reef walls and drop-offs close to shore.

02

A longer crossing than the other three routes — plan for most of the day on and around the water.

03

A mix of beginner-friendly shallow reefs and more advanced sites for experienced divers.

04

A genuinely quiet island outside the dive shops — low-rise, low-key, easy to like even if you never get in the water.

On the water

This route works best as a full-day charter — the crossing alone takes a real chunk of the day, so the value is in time spent at the island rather than rushing the trip out and back. It also pairs naturally with Koh Nang Yuan, just a short hop from Koh Tao's northwest coast, if you want two stops in one day.
